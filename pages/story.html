StudySmart Youth Services is a student-run nonprofit organization aimed to help K-8 students improve their academic achievements. The tutors of our program are dedicated high school volunteers who are passionate about knowledge and are willing to share our interests with young learners. It evolved from Math at the Library, founded in 2017. Through weekly free math tutoring sessions at various local libraries, Math at the Library help students with math homework or provide math enrichment materials for advanced learners. It has grown to encompass Code Champion and Girls Powering Girls, two other student volunteer organizations to provide computer coding instruction and English language tutoring to our community. We now offer online classes multiple times a week. We have more than 100 volunteers and provide free lessons to students with all background and social status.
