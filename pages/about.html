Arvin Ding

Co-founder

Portola High School, Grade 10

Hi I'm Arvin

I enjoy making friends, playing games, and talking. I like to have fun regardless of where I am or how. When teaching kids math, I begin to realize how wierd math is.

In the end its all made up by all of us. As you go deeper and deeper, it seems less and less pratical. Even at early stages of math such as addition, some math questions simply don't make any sense. Some of my favorite examples are of negative numbers and how some equations like 2-4=? simply cant be represented as a story. It doesn't make any sense to have 2 apples and eat 4 of them

Outside of debt or money, negative values have very little meaning. When teaching things such as addition or multiplication, its starts to become evident how much math is made up. It hard to answer many of questions for younger ones such as why we define 5+6 is 11. Many questions are hard or nearly impossible without memorizing it such as 8*8. Teaching is really fun in this aspect. In stead of seeing 2+2 as just 4 you begin to think about it like how many candies 2 freinds each with 2 candies have. You begin to think about how to explain the simpler overlooked things. The moment they begin to understand, is ultmately the most fun moment in teaching. When you see everything come together, its such a magical expirence for them and yourself as the teacher.
